Dhaka is the capital and largest city of Bangladesh. It is known as the heart of the country’s political, economic, and cultural activities. With a population of over 20 million, Dhaka is one of the most densely populated cities in the world. The city is famous for its rich history, vibrant street life, and delicious food. Important places in Dhaka include Lalbagh Fort, Ahsan Manzil, and the National Parliament House. It is also known as the "City of Rickshaws" because thousands of colorful cycle rickshaws run through its busy streets every day.

Chittagong is officially known as Chattogram, is the main port city of Bangladesh. Located on the southeastern coast, it is the second-largest city in the country. Chittagong is famous for its natural beauty, including hills, forests, and the Bay of Bengal. The city plays a major role in Bangladesh’s economy through its busy seaport, which handles most of the country's export and import activities.
